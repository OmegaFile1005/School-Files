Camelids

The four llamas and camels are camelids Camelids tend to be large and strictly herbivorous. Camelids differ from ruminanats in several ways. They have a three=chambered rather than a four-chambered digestive tract; an upper lip that is split in two with each part separately mobile; and an islolated incisor in the upper jaw. They have long legs that, because they lack tensor skin to bridge between thigh and body, look longer still. [Wikipedia]
